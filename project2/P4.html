I remember the fig tree twisting its powerful branches, its leaves stretching out like palms.
The fine gray hairs on the back of the leaves rub against each other, creating a canarylike texture under the flash light.
I once wondered if FIG leaves could also be sewed into clothes,
and I remember reading in the book that the first clothes Adam and Eve put on
after eating an apple in the Garden of Eden were FIG leaves.
From then on, I had a very intimate feeling for figs.
The flowers in the fruit,
the narrow entrance only for the FIG bee to enter,
and the soft and fuzzy leaves
all made me feel a certain intimate connection.
It's a very Chinese plant, with all the heat and tears inside, fermenting, purifying,
until it's finally pulled apart to release the softest sweetness
